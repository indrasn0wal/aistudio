# Adoption of 2025 Financial Plan for Arm of Springfield

## Summary
The special council meeting for the Arm of Springfield, held on April 29, 2025, focused on the adoption of the 2025 financial plan. Mayor Patrick Terriam presided, with all council members present. The agenda was adopted unanimously, and the main item discussed was the approval of the financial plan, which includes the operating and capital budgets, estimates of revenue and expenditures, and a five-year capital expenditure program. 

Council members raised concerns about the significant property tax increases resulting from the provincial reassessment, with some residents facing up to a 21% hike. There was a robust discussion about the lack of public engagement mechanisms, the use of additional revenue from reassessment, and the need for fiscal restraint. Despite dissent from Councillors Miller and Kaczynski, who advocated for more frugality and public transparency, the financial plan was adopted by majority vote. The meeting concluded with acknowledgments of the administration's efforts and a call for continued fiscal responsibility.

## Key Points
### Adoption of 2025 Financial Plan *(importance: 10/10)*
- The council reviewed and voted on the 2025 financial plan, which includes the operating budget, capital budget, revenue and expenditure estimates, and a five-year capital expenditure program.
### Concerns Over Property Tax Increases *(importance: 9/10)*
- Several councillors expressed concern about significant property tax increases (averaging 17-18%, with some as high as 21%) due to provincial reassessment, and the impact on residents' affordability.
### Public Engagement and Transparency *(importance: 8/10)*
- Discussion highlighted the lack of mechanisms for public input at special meetings, with councillors noting frustration from residents about limited opportunities to engage or have their concerns addressed.
### Use of Additional Revenue from Reassessment *(importance: 7/10)*
- There was debate about the council's approach to spending the extra $3.3 million from reassessment, with calls for restraint and prioritization of essential projects over discretionary spending.
### Budget Review Process *(importance: 6/10)*
- The mayor and council emphasized that the budget process was thorough, involving detailed line-by-line reviews, input from directors, and efforts to reduce the mill rate where possible.
### Acknowledgment of Administrative Efforts *(importance: 5/10)*
- Council members recognized the hard work of administration and staff in preparing the budget and managing taxpayer funds responsibly.
